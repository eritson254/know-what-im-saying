Somewhere along the way, most of us learned to translate "they didn't choose me" into "I am not enough" — as if the first sentence were simply a more polite way of saying the second.

It isn't. But the brain doesn't always know the difference.

## The Psychology

Neuroimaging studies have found that social rejection activates some of the same regions involved in processing physical pain. Your nervous system is not exaggerating. It just isn't always pointed at the right conclusion.
