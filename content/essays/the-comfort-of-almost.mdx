There is a particular kind of relationship that never quite becomes one — and the waiting, it turns out, carries a quiet cost we rarely name.

It lives in the space between a held glance and a sent text — the warmth of someone's attention without the weight of their commitment.

> We stay for who they occasionally let us believe they might become.

A clear rejection ends something. Ambiguity keeps the door propped open just enough to keep you standing in the hallway.

## The Psychology

Intermittent reinforcement — reward that arrives unpredictably — produces stronger, stickier attachment than consistent reward does. It is the same mechanism that makes a slot machine more compelling than a vending machine.
