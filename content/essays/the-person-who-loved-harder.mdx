> "I kept calling it love because I did not yet know what to call carrying a connection alone."

There is a particular kind of tiredness that comes from loving someone in ways they may never fully notice.

It does not arrive all at once.

At first, it feels natural to be thoughtful. You remember the thing they mentioned in passing. You check in before their difficult meeting. You notice when their replies sound different. You make room for their bad days because you know life can be heavy, and you care enough to want them to feel less alone in it.

You tell yourself that this is what love asks of people.

**Attention. Patience. Grace.**

So you give it.

You give it when they are stressed and distracted. You give it when they come to you with a problem they have carried all day. You give it when they need reassurance, when they need someone to listen, when they need a soft place to land after life has been unkind to them.

You give it because their pain matters to you.

I gave it because *theirs* mattered to me.

For a long time, I did not see myself as someone who was doing too much. I saw myself as someone who loved deeply. I felt proud of how much I could understand without being told. I knew when to give space. I knew when to ask questions gently. I knew how to make difficult moments feel lighter. I knew how to hold back my own disappointment when they were already dealing with something.

I became very good at making things easier for them.

**Somewhere in that, I stopped asking whether anything was becoming easier for me.**

## I became the person who remembered

I remembered the details.

I remembered when they had something important coming up. I remembered stories about their family. I remembered the things that made them insecure, the things they wanted to change about their life, the dreams they only spoke about when they were feeling hopeful.

I remembered because I cared.

Sometimes I would hear a song, see a place, find something funny online, and immediately think of them. I would save it for later. I would picture the small smile they might have when they saw it. There was so much of my day that naturally reached toward them.

They were woven into ordinary moments.

I would be walking home and want to tell them something that had happened. I would be in a shop and see something they would like. I would make plans in my head and quietly leave space for them inside those plans.

**They had become part of the shape of my life before we had ever agreed on what we were.**

That was one of the hardest things to admit.

I had given them a place in my future because I believed the connection was growing. I thought the closeness meant we were moving toward something. I thought the tenderness between us was leading somewhere, even when nobody said where.

I kept reading the small moments as signs.

A message at the right time.

A late-night call.

A conversation where they let their guard down.

A rare moment of openness that made me feel as though I had finally reached them.

I held onto those moments tightly. They carried more weight than they should have because I needed them to mean something.

> "I mistook the moments when they reached for me for proof that they were walking toward me."

## I was always trying to understand

There were times when they pulled away.

Sometimes it happened quietly. Replies became shorter. Plans stopped forming. The energy shifted in a way I could feel before I had words for it. I would sense the distance and immediately begin looking for reasons.

Maybe they were overwhelmed.

Maybe something was happening at work.

Maybe they were having a difficult week.

Maybe they needed space.

Maybe I had said something wrong.

That last one found its way into my mind more often than I want to admit.

I became someone who could explain almost anything for them. I could find the soft reason behind the silence. I could make their distance feel understandable. I could take a confusing situation and wrap it in empathy until it hurt less.

I was protecting them in conversations they were not even present for.

Friends would ask me how things were going, and I would pause before answering. There was always a way to make it sound better than it felt. I would mention the sweet things. I would mention how busy they had been. I would mention the conversation we had last week when everything seemed good again.

I rarely spoke about the waiting.

I rarely admitted how often I checked my phone.

I rarely said that I had started measuring the quality of my day by whether I had heard from them.

I did not want to sound dramatic. I did not want anyone to tell me what I already suspected. I wanted to believe that being understanding would eventually make everything feel steady.

Instead, I became more careful.

I began thinking too long before sending a message. I questioned whether I was asking for too much. I kept parts of myself quiet because I did not want my feelings to become another thing they had to deal with.

**The connection had started asking me to shrink in small ways.**

I did not see it clearly then.

## Loving hard can make you loyal to a version of someone

There is a point where love becomes tied to potential.

You stop relating only to the person in front of you. You begin holding space for the person you believe they could become when life settles down, when fear loosens its grip, when they have healed enough to receive what you are offering.

I spent a long time loving the possibility of us.

I loved the moments that made me think they were capable of choosing me fully. I loved the parts of them that appeared when they felt safe. I loved the version of the connection that existed during the good days.

Those good days were beautiful.

They were also difficult to carry after everything changed again.

I would replay them when I was hurt. I would return to them when I was trying to make sense of another silence. I would use them as evidence that the closeness was real, even when the rest of the connection left me confused.

It was real to *me*.

That is why it mattered.

I do not think loving harder means someone is weak. I think it can come from a generous heart. It can come from someone who knows what it feels like to be misunderstood and wants to offer others the grace they once needed themselves.

Still, love can become lonely when one person is always doing the emotional work.

One person notices the change in tone.

One person reaches out after the argument.

One person finds the words for what is happening.

One person keeps believing there is something worth saving.

> Eventually, the love begins to feel like a job nobody asked you to do.

## I confused being needed with being chosen

I was needed often.

They came to me when life felt difficult. They reached for me when they were lonely, tired, overwhelmed, unsure. I knew how to listen without making them feel judged. I knew how to make room for their feelings.

Being needed can feel intimate.

It can make you believe you are important in someone's life. It can make you feel close to them in a way that looks like love from the inside.

I held onto that feeling.

I thought that because they trusted me with their hard moments, they must have been building something with me. I thought my presence in their difficult seasons meant I had a place in the future they were creating.

I had not asked them.

I had simply hoped.

*There is a difference between being someone's comfort and being someone's commitment.* I have learned that slowly.

A person can lean on you deeply and still leave you carrying your own pain alone.

A person can need your warmth and still never make you feel safe.

A person can tell you things nobody else knows and still keep the most important truth from you.

> The truth being that you are more invested than they are.

That truth is hard to sit with.

It makes you look back at every kind moment with new eyes. It makes you wonder whether you misunderstood the whole thing. It makes you question your memory, your instincts, your ability to read people.

I have done all of that.

I have wondered whether I created more meaning than was ever there.

I have wondered whether I was too hopeful.

I have wondered whether I loved a little too openly in a world where people are rewarded for holding back.

I am still learning how to answer those questions with kindness.

## The quiet moment of realising

There was no dramatic event that showed me I loved harder.

Nobody said it directly.

There was no single conversation where everything became clear.

It arrived in smaller moments.

It arrived when I realised I could tell you everything they were going through, yet I was unsure whether they would notice when I was struggling.

It arrived when I saw how easily I rearranged my day for them.

It arrived when I noticed I had become afraid of being honest because I did not want to risk losing the little I had.

It arrived when I understood that I had been spending more time trying to keep the connection alive than actually feeling loved inside it.

That realisation sat with me for a while.

I did not know what to do with it at first.

I still cared. I still hoped. I still wanted to believe that all the love I had given would eventually be met with the same tenderness.

Part of me still wants that.

The other part of me has started asking harder questions.

*What does love feel like when it is shared?*

*What would change if I stopped carrying every difficult moment alone?*

*Who would I be if I did not have to work so hard to feel close to someone?*

I do not have all the answers yet.

I only know that **I am tired of calling exhaustion devotion.**

**I am tired of confusing anxiety with depth.**

I am tired of thinking that loving someone well requires me to keep accepting a version of love that leaves me alone with too much.

I loved them.

I may still love them for a while.

That does not mean I have to keep losing myself inside the feeling.

> "Love should have room for both people to exist fully inside it."

This is where I am now.

I am learning to notice when I am carrying too much.

I am learning that care should move in more than one direction.

**I am learning that being the person who loves harder can feel beautiful for a while, until you realise how little of that love has ever made its way back to you.**
