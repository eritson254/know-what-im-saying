## Independence

*Know What I'm Saying?* is written and edited independently. There is no sponsor, publisher, or advertiser shaping what gets covered. If that ever changes (if a piece is sponsored or a product link is an affiliate link), it will be disclosed clearly, both in the piece itself and on the [Disclosures](/disclosures) page.

## How reader stories become essays

Stories submitted through [Share Your Story](/share-your-story) are read with care, not guaranteed publication, and never published as-is. If a story is adapted into an essay, identifying details are changed or removed, and the result is a reflection inspired by the submission rather than a verbatim retelling. Submitting a story is never a substitute for a response, and we don't promise to follow up with everyone who writes in.

## Pseudonymous authorship

The writing here is published under "the editor" rather than a personal byline. That choice is intentional: it keeps the focus on the writing and the reader's own experience rather than on a personality. It does not mean less accountability: corrections, questions, and feedback are always welcome through the contact page.

## What this writing is and isn't

Essays and notes here are personal, reflective writing, not journalism, not clinical research, and not professional advice. When psychology or behavioral concepts are referenced, they're offered as general, educational framing, not a diagnosis of you or anyone else. If something written here resonates, that's the point; if it doesn't match your situation, that's expected too.

## Corrections

If something published here is factually wrong, or a fair description of a situation is requested by someone whose story was adapted, write to hello@knowwhatimsaying.com. Corrections are made transparently rather than quietly edited away.
