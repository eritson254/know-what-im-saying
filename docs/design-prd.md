# Design PRD
## Know What I’m Saying?

**Project:** Know What I’m Saying?  
**Document type:** Website Design Product Requirements Document  
**Audience:** UI/UX designers, visual designers, and developers  
**Platform:** Static editorial website  
**Primary use case:** Long-form reading, story-led essay series, reader submissions, community growth, and future digital products  
**Tone:** Chill, intelligent, intimate, editorial, and professional

---

# 1. Design Objective

Design a premium editorial website for **Know What I’m Saying?**, a publication about life, psychology, love, relationships, technology, money, work, culture, and modern identity.

The website should not feel like a generic blog, a personal portfolio, a self-help site, or a content-heavy media website.

It should feel like a calm, thoughtful digital publication built for people who still enjoy reading.

The experience should communicate:

- Depth
- Emotional intelligence
- Curiosity
- Modernity
- Calm confidence
- Warmth
- Strong storytelling
- Editorial credibility

The design should make readers feel like they have entered a thoughtful corner of the internet where they can slow down.

---

# 2. Brand Feeling

The visual direction should feel like:

> A late-night conversation with the smartest person in the room who is not trying to prove they are the smartest person in the room.

Keywords:

- Editorial
- Literary
- Reflective
- Minimal
- Human
- Intelligent
- Warm
- Calm
- Contemporary
- Premium
- Slightly conversational
- Thoughtful
- Clean
- Unhurried

Avoid:

- Generic self-help aesthetics
- Loud startup gradients
- Corporate SaaS layouts
- Overuse of cards
- Heavy shadows
- Bright influencer colors
- Excessive animations
- Busy article pages
- Overly feminine or overly masculine styling
- Stock-photo-heavy layouts
- Dark, gloomy “therapy blog” aesthetics
- Over-designed magazine layouts that make reading difficult

---

# 3. Design Philosophy

The writing is the product.

Every design decision should support reading, discovery, trust, and return visits.

The site should be visually polished, but the design must never compete with the essays.

The hierarchy should always be:

1. Story / idea
2. Title
3. Reading experience
4. Series context
5. Related content
6. Community or newsletter CTA
7. Products or monetization

The site should feel spacious. It should not rush the reader.

# 6. Global Layout System

## 6.1 Site Widths

Recommended desktop content widths:

```text
Full page max width: 1440px
Standard content container: 1180px–1240px
Article reading column: 680px–760px
Wide article visual / hero area: 960px–1100px
Sidebar / related content column: 280px–340px
```

The article body must remain narrow enough for long-form reading.

## 6.2 Spacing

The site should use generous vertical spacing.

Recommended visual rhythm:

- Large hero spacing
- Comfortable distance between article sections
- Room around headings
- High line-height in articles
- Clear visual separation between editorial modules

Avoid compressed layouts.

## 6.3 Grid System

Desktop:
- 12-column grid
- Clear gutters
- Flexible editorial layouts

Tablet:
- 8-column grid

Mobile:
- Single-column reading layout
- Horizontal scrolling only where intentionally used for category chips or series cards

# 7. Visual Identity Direction

## 7.1 Color Direction

Primary background:
- Warm off-white
- Soft paper tone
- Very light cream
- Optional subtle texture

Primary text:
- Deep charcoal
- Soft black
- Dark warm gray

Accent colors:
Choose one primary accent and one secondary support tone.

Suggested directions:

### Option A: Warm Editorial
- Warm cream background
- Charcoal text
- Burnt orange accent
- Dusty rose or muted clay support tone

### Option B: Calm Literary
- Soft ivory background
- Deep charcoal text
- Forest green accent
- Muted sage support tone

### Option C: Modern Intellectual
- Soft gray-white background
- Ink black text
- Muted cobalt blue accent
- Pale blue-gray support tone

### Option D: Rich Reflective
- Warm neutral background
- Deep brown-black text
- Burgundy / wine accent
- Pale blush support tone

The final color system must meet accessibility contrast standards.

## 7.2 Typography

The site should combine an editorial serif with a clean modern sans-serif.

Recommended pairing:

```text
Headlines / large editorial text: Instrument Serif or DM Serif Display
Body text: Geist, Inter, Manrope, Source Sans 3, or IBM Plex Sans
Metadata / category labels: Geist Mono or small uppercase sans-serif
```

Typography should create contrast between:
- Emotional, literary article titles
- Clear readable body copy
- Quiet metadata

### Type hierarchy

```text
Display headline: 64px–92px desktop / 42px–58px mobile
Hero supporting text: 20px–24px desktop / 18px–20px mobile
Article title: 48px–72px desktop / 36px–48px mobile
Article body: 18px–21px desktop / 17px–19px mobile
Article line-height: 1.65–1.85
Section heading: 28px–40px desktop
Metadata: 12px–14px
Navigation: 14px–16px
```

## 7.3 Imagery

Use imagery carefully.

Recommended:
- Editorial photography
- Abstract visual compositions
- Quiet cinematic images
- Textural backgrounds
- Thoughtful illustration
- Minimal symbolic objects
- Cropped environmental photography
- Occasional black-and-white photography
- Minimal portrait photography where relevant

Avoid:
- Generic stock photos of people at laptops
- Overly literal visual metaphors
- Repeated sad faces for emotional essays
- Highly saturated lifestyle photos
- Random decorative imagery that adds no meaning

Article visuals should feel intentional and help set the tone.

```text
Know What I’m Saying?

Thoughtful writing for people trying to make sense of modern life.

Essays on psychology, love, money, technology, culture, ambition, and the stories people carry.
```

- Lots of whitespace
- Optional subtle paper texture

#### Section 3: Featured Essay

Layout:
- Large image or visual block
- Topic label
- Large title
- Excerpt
- Date and reading time
- CTA

Mobile:

#### Section 4: Latest Writing

Layout:
- Section heading

#### Section 7: Reader Story CTA

Layout:
- Soft contrast section

A vertical, clearly numbered sequence.


Required:
- Topic label
- Series context if relevant

- Subtitle / deck
- Publish date

- Optional author name
- Share actions

Suggested layout:
- Narrow centered article header
- Strong title typography
- Metadata visually quiet

#### Section 3: Series Navigation Bar

Only for series content.

Should include:
- Series title
- Essay number
- Progress indicator
- “Start from Essay One”

Desktop:

Requirements:

- Comfortable line-height
- Large body font
- Clear H2 / H3 hierarchy
- Pull quote styling
- Optional full-bleed images
- Optional footnotes / source area
- Optional inline definition blocks
- Optional “pause and reflect” callouts

Optional and only when contextually appropriate.

Examples:
- Newsletter signup
- WhatsApp Channel
- Product recommendation
- Reader story CTA

#### Section 6: Closing Reflection

Optional visual treatment:
- Larger final paragraph

Layout:
- 3 related article cards
- Prefer same topic or series

# 10. Mobile Design Requirements

Mobile is essential because a large portion of readers may arrive from TikTok, WhatsApp, Instagram, Reddit, or search.

## Mobile priorities

- Comfortable reading width
- Large readable text
- No tiny metadata
- Easy article sharing
- Clear series navigation
- Fast page load
- Minimal sticky elements
- Accessible menu
- No cluttered card grids
- Popups must not obstruct reading

## Mobile article behavior

- Article title should remain strong but not oversized
- Body text must not feel cramped
- Related essays should stack vertically
- Series progress should be compact
- Share tools should be simple
- Newsletter/WhatsApp CTA should fit naturally below content
- Full-screen mobile menu should feel premium and uncluttered

---

# 11. Interaction and Motion Design

Motion should be subtle and calm.

Recommended:
- Fade-in on section entry
- Soft hover changes on cards
- Underline transitions on links
- Gentle image scaling on hover
- Subtle header transition on scroll
- Modal fade/slide
- Reading progress movement

Avoid:
- Excessive scroll animation
- Bouncy interactions
- Fast, distracting transitions
- Animated text that delays reading
- Auto-playing media
- Heavy parallax

The writing should remain the focus.

---

# 12. Accessibility Requirements

The final design must support:

- Strong color contrast
- Keyboard navigation
- Clear focus states
- Readable type sizes
- Responsive text scaling
- Alt text support
- Semantic content hierarchy
- Reduced-motion settings
- Clear form labels
- Accessible modal behavior
- Mobile usability
- No information communicated only by color

# 17. Final Design Standard

The final website should make a reader feel:

- “This is different from the usual internet.”
- “I can stay here for a while.”
- “This person understands something I have struggled to explain.”
- “I want to read another piece.”
- “I trust this publication.”
- “I want to come back when the next essay is published.”

The website should be beautiful enough to feel premium, simple enough to feel calm, and thoughtful enough to match the quality of the writing.

> The design should not chase attention.  
> It should reward it.
